Ice Water Dispenser (IWD)

The Stationary Ice Water Dispenser is a digitally controlled, solar-powered chilling system that produces and stores food-grade glycol-cooled water. This chilled water is used to cool milk, fish, and other perishables through closed-loop cold packs (ladder packs or thermocores). It acts as a mini cold chain anchor for farmers, fisherfolk, and vendors with limited access to full refrigeration.

Key Features

  • Digital Dispensing System: Select amount via touch interface or app
  • Metered Delivery: 1–20L by volume with pre/post payment (M-Pesa compatible)
  • Insulated Glycol Tank: 100L chilling tank stores glycol-cooled water at stable temps
  • Recirculation Loop: Used glycol water is returned, re-cooled, and reused
  • Separate tanks: 100L (chilled) + 200L (warm return)
  • Low-Cost Access: Pay-per-use model for inclusive access

Frequently Asked Questions

How is water dispensed?

Users select the required volume (1–20L) on the touchscreen or through a connected app. The system meters delivery and supports prepaid or postpaid billing, including mobile money (M-Pesa).

What is the cooling medium?

Food-grade glycol-chilled water is stored in a 100L cold tank. Used return water flows back to a 200L warm tank and is re-cooled for reuse in a closed loop.

Does it run off-grid?

Yes. The system is solar-powered and designed for off-grid or grid-limited environments common in rural dairy and fisheries ecosystems.

Which products are compatible?

Nomad Cans with thermocores, EcoSav Bags with ladder packs, and Nomad EV/D mobile dispensers are built to integrate with IWD hubs. Data can sync with MaziwaPlus DMS.

Is there a pay-per-use option?

Yes. The IWD supports inclusive, low-cost access through pay-per-use metering.
